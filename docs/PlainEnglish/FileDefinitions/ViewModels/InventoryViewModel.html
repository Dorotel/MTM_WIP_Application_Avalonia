  • üì¶ What parts we have (part numbers, descriptions)

üè≠ Real-World Manufacturing Example

Scenario: Production Supervisor Checking Stock

Lisa, the production supervisor, starts her shift and needs to check what parts are available for today's production runs.

1

Open Inventory View

Lisa clicks on the "Inventory" tab to see the main inventory display screen. The system loads all current stock information.

2

She searches for "BEARING" to see all bearing parts. The list filters to show only bearing-related inventory.

3

Lisa sees that "BEARING-001" has only 5 units left at Operation 100, which is below the minimum. The row shows in red to indicate low stock.

4

She checks where the bearings are located and sees they're split between "BIN-A12" (3 units) and "WORKSTATION-05" (2 units).

5

Take Action

From the inventory screen, Lisa can directly order more bearings or move existing stock to where it's needed most.

Stock Level Classifications

  • üö® Critical (Red): Less than 10 units or below minimum threshold
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Low (Yellow): Between minimum and reorder point
  • ‚úÖ Good (Green): Above reorder point with adequate safety stock

Access Controls

  • Floor Supervisors: Can initiate stock moves and adjustments

üîß Common Issues & Solutions

‚ùì "I can't find a part I know we have"

Solution: Try searching with partial part numbers or descriptions. Check if it might be assigned to a different operation than expected.

‚ùì "The quantities look wrong"

Solution: Click the refresh button to get the latest data. If still incorrect, check if there are pending transactions being processed.

‚ùì "The screen is loading slowly"

Solution: Use filters to reduce the amount of data displayed. Large inventory lists take longer to load and display.

‚ùì "I need to find parts in a specific location"

Solution: Use the Location filter dropdown to show only items in that location, or search for the location name in the search box.
