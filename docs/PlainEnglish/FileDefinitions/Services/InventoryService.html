üè≠ What This Service Does in MTM Manufacturing

The Inventory Service is like the warehouse manager for the entire MTM operation. It keeps track of every part, material, and finished product in the factory - knowing exactly how many of each item you have, where they're located, and what manufacturing stage they're at.

üì¶ Parts Tracking
Monitors every individual part and component used in manufacturing, from raw materials to finished products.
üìç Location Management
Keeps track of where each part is stored in the warehouse or what workstation it's currently at.
üîÑ Operation Tracking
Knows what manufacturing step each part is at (like Operation 90, 100, or 110 in the MTM process).
üìä Quantity Control
Maintains accurate counts of how many of each part you have available for production.

üéØ What This Service Actually Does

Think of this service as your digital warehouse assistant. It handles all the day-to-day tasks that keep track of your inventory, just like a warehouse supervisor would, but much faster and more accurately.

Main Responsibilities

  • Stock Monitoring: Always knows exactly what you have in stock
  • Movement Tracking: Records when parts move between locations or operations
  • Adding New Items: Properly registers new parts when they arrive
  • Removing Items: Correctly removes parts when they're used or shipped
  • Location Searches: Quickly finds items based on where they're stored
  • Operation Queries: Shows what parts are at specific manufacturing stages
Important: This service is critical for production - without accurate inventory tracking, you can't plan manufacturing schedules or know when to order more materials.

üîß Key Functions Explained Simply

üìã Get All Inventory
Shows you a complete list of everything in your warehouse - every part, how many you have, and where they're located. Like taking a full inventory count instantly.
Real-world example: "Show me every part we have in stock right now, organized by part number and manufacturing operation."
üîç Find Specific Part
Looks up a specific part by its ID number and tells you everything about it - quantity, location, manufacturing stage, and when it was last updated.
Real-world example: "I need to find part WIDGET-001. Where is it, how many do we have, and what operation stage is it at?"
‚ûï Add New Parts
Registers new parts when they arrive at the facility. Makes sure all the information is correct (like part number, quantity, location) before adding them to the system.
Real-world example: "We just received 50 units of part ABC-123. Add them to the system at location WH-A1, operation 90."
üìù Update Part Information
Changes information about existing parts - like moving them to a new location, updating quantities, or changing their manufacturing operation stage.
Real-world example: "Part XYZ-789 has moved from operation 90 to operation 100, and its location changed from WH-A1 to WS-3."
üìà Add Stock (Receiving)
Records when more quantity of an existing part is added to inventory. This happens when you receive shipments or when manufacturing creates more of a part.
Real-world example: "We just manufactured 25 more units of part DEF-456. Add these to our existing stock at workstation WS-2."
üìâ Remove Stock (Using/Shipping)
Records when parts are taken out of inventory - either used in manufacturing or shipped to customers. Reduces the quantity and tracks who removed them.
Real-world example: "Production used 15 units of part GHI-789 from location WH-B2 for today's manufacturing run."
üîÑ Transfer Between Locations
Moves parts from one location to another without changing the total quantity. Like moving parts from the warehouse to a workstation for manufacturing.
Real-world example: "Move 10 units of part JKL-012 from warehouse storage WH-C1 to manufacturing workstation WS-5."
üìç Search by Location
Shows all parts stored at a specific location. Useful for organizing warehouse areas or knowing what's available at each workstation.
Real-world example: "Show me everything stored in warehouse section WH-A so I can organize that area."
üîß Search by Operation
Shows all parts that are at a specific manufacturing operation stage. Helps with production planning and workflow management.
Real-world example: "Show me all parts currently at operation 100 so I can plan tomorrow's production schedule."
